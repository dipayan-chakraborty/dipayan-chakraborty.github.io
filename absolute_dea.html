Absolute DEA Cross-Section Measurements

Research Overview

This research focuses on the development of experimental techniques for measuring absolute dissociative electron attachment (DEA) cross-sections in isolated gas-phase molecular systems. These measurements provide quantitative insight into electron-driven fragmentation dynamics and transient negative ion formation.

Special emphasis is placed on the design and optimization of electron collision apparatus, ion extraction optics, time-of-flight mass spectrometry, and calibration methodologies required for reliable absolute cross-section measurements. These studies are important for understanding fundamental electron-induced processes relevant to plasma physics, radiation chemistry, atmospheric chemistry, and astrochemistry.
