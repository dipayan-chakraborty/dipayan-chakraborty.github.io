Velocity Map Imaging (VMI) Studies

Research Overview

Velocity map imaging (VMI) techniques are employed to investigate the dynamics of low-energy electron interactions with isolated molecules. These studies provide detailed information about fragmentation pathways, kinetic energy release distributions, and angular scattering processes associated with dissociative electron attachment.

Our work focuses on understanding the role of transient negative ion resonances in driving molecular fragmentation dynamics. Experimental measurements are combined with imaging analysis to obtain insight into electron-induced processes relevant to fundamental AMO physics, radiation chemistry, and molecular reaction dynamics.
